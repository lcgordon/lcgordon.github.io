About

I'm an incoming first year astrophysics graduate student at UMN. I'll be working with Tom Jones on MHD simulations and the WOMBAT team optimizing for HPC. My research interests are in computational and data science focused astronomy, and my past work has ranged from particle accelerators to exoplanets to supernovae.

I attended Wellesley College (c/o 2021) and graduated cum laude with my B.A. in Astrophysics with Honors and a minor in Computer Science. Previously I've worked as a researcher at Lowell Observatory, Whitin Observatory, Middlebury College, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, and MIT's Kavli Center for Astrophysics and Space Research. For a complete list of my experience, you can check out my resume or my research page.

Mergen: An Unsupervised Pipeline for TESS Light Curve Classification

  • January 2020 -- present. Advisor: Tansu Daylan (MIT)

  • Public Python framework package and 2+ papers in progress.
